NYC ZONING DISTRICTS — QUICK REFERENCE
(For development-site analysis. FAR = Floor Area Ratio = buildable floor area per sq ft of lot.)

=== RESIDENCE DISTRICTS ===

R1 / R2: Lowest-density. Single-family detached homes. Max residential FAR 0.5.

R3: Low-density. Detached/semi-detached one- and two-family homes. FAR ~0.5-0.6.

R4: Low-density. Small multi-family and row houses. FAR ~0.75.

R5: Low/medium density. FAR ~1.25.

R6: Medium-density. Most common in outer-borough development. Height Factor FAR 0.78-2.43; Quality Housing up to 2.43-3.0.

R6A: Medium-density CONTEXTUAL district. Max residential FAR 3.0. Quality Housing program. Common in Astoria, Brooklyn infill. Height capped (~70 ft).

R6B: Lower contextual. Max FAR 2.0. Brownstone-scale, ~50 ft height limit.

R7: Medium/high density. Height Factor FAR up to 3.44; Quality Housing higher.

R7A: Contextual. Max FAR 4.0. Common in transit-rich corridors. ~80 ft height.

R7X: Contextual. Max FAR 5.0. Higher-density contextual; emerging dev corridors (LIC, Bronx).

R8: High-density. FAR up to 6.02 (Height Factor) / 6.02-7.2 (Quality Housing).

R9 / R10: Highest residential density. R10 max FAR ~10.0. Manhattan and dense cores.

Note: Inclusionary Housing and other bonuses can increase residential FAR above the base
(e.g. R6A with IH can reach ~3.6). Always verify against the current Zoning Resolution.

=== COMMERCIAL DISTRICTS ===

C1 / C2: Local retail OVERLAYS mapped over residential districts (e.g. C1-3, C2-4).
The number after the dash sets parking/use; residential FAR follows the underlying R district.

C4: Regional commercial centers outside the CBD. Specialty retail, offices, residential.
Commercial FAR typically 3.4; residential equivalent varies by suffix (C4-2 ~ R6 residential,
C4-3 ~ R6, C4-4/4-5 higher). Common for mixed-use development sites.

C4-2: Lower-density general commercial. Commercial FAR 3.4, residential FAR ~2.43 (R6 equiv).
Mixed-use as-of-right. Frequent in Queens/Brooklyn commercial corridors.

C5 / C6: High-density commercial. C6 includes the densest mixed-use (Midtown, Downtown).
Commercial FAR up to 10-15 with bonuses.

C8: Heavy commercial / automotive. Generally no residential as-of-right.

=== MANUFACTURING DISTRICTS ===

M1: Light manufacturing. Most retail/offices as-of-right. Residential generally prohibited
(except MX / special mixed-use districts). FAR ~1.0-5.0 depending on suffix.

M1-2 / M1-4 / M1-5: Light industrial; higher suffixes allow higher commercial FAR.
Increasingly targeted for rezoning to residential/mixed-use.

M2 / M3: Medium and heavy manufacturing. No residential. Waterfront/industrial.

MX (Special Mixed Use): Pairs an M1 with a residential district (e.g. M1-2/R6A) to allow
both residential and light-industrial — common in rezoned creative/industrial neighborhoods.

=== KEY CONCEPTS FOR SITE VALUATION ===

BUILDABLE SQUARE FOOTAGE (BSF) = Lot Area x Residential FAR (as-of-right).
Example: 5,000 sf lot in R6A (FAR 3.0) = 15,000 BSF as-of-right.

AS-OF-RIGHT: What can be built without special permits or variances.

SPECIAL DISTRICTS: Overlay custom rules on base zoning (e.g. Special Long Island City
Mixed Use District, Special Hudson Yards District). Always check spdist fields.

CITY OF YES (2024-2025): Citywide zoning reforms expanding residential development capacity,
including the Universal Affordability Preference (UAP) bonus and reduced parking mandates.
